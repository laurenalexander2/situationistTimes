Introduction

In the years between 1962 and 1967, Dutch artist Jacqueline de Jong edited and issued six issues of The Situationist Times. This was a collaborative, multi-lingual, and multi-disciplinary journal. It featured a diverse array of contributions, including essays, poems, photographs, collages, and comics, and it covered a wide range of topics – urbanism, consumerism, art, and politics. The journal synthesized threads of varied intellectual disciplines into a coherent critique against advanced capitalism and its inherent injustices.

Jacqueline de Jong

Jacqueline de Jong’s art embodies revolutionary aesthetics and politics, blending figuration and narrative to create archetypal depictions of human suffering and strife. As a WWII émigrée, her work reflects on trauma and powerlessness, yet resists fixed interpretations.

Following her expulsion from the Situationist International movement, De Jong founded The Situationist Times and engaged in local activism, crafting disruptive public interventions. Her prolific art, marked by fractured compositions, explores cruelty, humor, and the human condition, resulting in a uniquely affecting body of work.
